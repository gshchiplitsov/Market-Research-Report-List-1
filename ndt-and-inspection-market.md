<p><p>NDT, or Non-Destructive Testing, is a technique used to examine materials and components for defects or irregularities without causing any damage. The NDT market can be categorized into several types of inspection methods. Ultrasonic Testing employs sound waves to detect flaws or measure material thickness. Visual Testing involves visual examination for surface flaws. Magnetic Particle Testing uses magnetic fields and iron particles to locate defects. Liquid Penetrant Testing applies a dye to identify surface cracks or leaks. Eddy-Current Testing locates flaws using electromagnetic induction. Radiographic Testing employs X-rays or gamma rays. Acoustic Emission Testing listens for stress-induced emissions. Additionally, there are other NDT methods currently being developed or niche markets emerging in this industry.</p></p>
