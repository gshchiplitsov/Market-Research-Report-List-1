<p><p>Mobile Digital Radiography (DR) refers to the use of portable X-ray systems that can be easily transported and operated at the patient's bedside or any other location within the healthcare facility. DR technology enables immediate image acquisition and transmission to a computer system, eliminating the need for traditional film-based radiography and improving efficiency in the diagnostic process.</p><p>The current outlook of the Mobile Digital Radiography (DR) market is highly favorable, driven by various factors. Firstly, there is an increasing demand for portable imaging devices due to the growing geriatric population and the rise in chronic diseases requiring frequent imaging. Additionally, advancements in technology have led to the development of lightweight, user-friendly, and cost-effective mobile DR systems, driving their adoption in healthcare facilities. The ability to provide immediate results and reduce patient waiting time is also contributing to the market's growth.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has further accelerated the adoption of mobile DR systems as they allow for point-of-care imaging, reducing the risk of cross-contamination in hospitals. The rise in telemedicine services also creates opportunities for mobile DR devices, as they facilitate remote diagnosis and monitoring.</p><p>The future outlook of the Mobile Digital Radiography (DR) market is expected to be promising. The market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.8% during the forecasted period. Factors such as technological advancements, increasing healthcare infrastructure in developing countries, and the rising demand for point-of-care imaging are expected to drive market growth. Additionally, the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms in mobile DR systems for automated image analysis and improved diagnosis is likely to boost market expansion in the future. However, challenges such as high cost and concerns regarding radiation exposure need to be addressed for sustained market growth.</p></p>

Smaller pixel sizes, like 100 micron, provide higher spatial resolution, allowing for more detailed images but may have lower sensitivity. On the other hand, larger pixel sizes, like 150 micron, offer better sensitivity but may sacrifice some image detail. "Others" denotes any pixel size not specifically mentioned.</p></p>
